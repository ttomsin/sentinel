## Why Sentinel Exists

It started with YouTube.

I kept watching video after video of developers — real developers, talented people who had spent years building things — genuinely angry. Angry about their work being scraped, trained on, and turned into tools that now compete with them. No credit. No compensation. No conversation.

Then I saw what happened to TailwindCSS. One of the most recognised, most used CSS tools in the world — and they're struggling to profit because AI knows their entire API, their entire implementation, their entire way of doing things. A tool that hundreds of thousands of developers rely on daily. And AI just... absorbed it. I don't know the full story. But I know enough.

That's when it stopped being background noise for me.

How can one of the most famous tools in the world not be able to profit from its own work because of AI? These aren't abstract statistics. These are people. These are developers who sacrificed nights and weekends and sanity building things the rest of us use every day.

I got tired of watching it happen and not seeing a technical response. So I built one.

## What Sentinel Is and Isn't

**Sentinel IS:**
- A real tool that encrypts your code before it reaches GitHub
- A real blockchain-anchored proof of authorship system
- A real AI interrogation tool that produced 61-66% similarity scores on real JWT code in real testing
- Open source — completely, unconditionally open source
- Unencrypted — Sentinel's own code is fully readable by anyone, including AI. It should be. The world should be able to read and improve it.

**Sentinel IS NOT:**
- A guaranteed legal victory
- A perfect solution
- Finished
- Mine alone to finish

## On Making Money

I don't know yet.

My honest answer is: I don't know if Sentinel will ever be a business. I'm not sure I want it to be. What I want is for it to work — for it to actually make a difference in the conversation between developers and AI companies.

Big AI companies think they can take our source code, train on it, use it to build products, and then dismiss the people whose work made those products possible. That's what this is about. Not a business model. Not a monetisation strategy.

For all the good developers out there whose code has been scraped and used without permission — this tool is for them. It's time to hold AI companies to account. Not with anger. With evidence.

## The Motto

> *"The fact that it knows shows a likelihood that it trained on my code."*

This was the insight that made Phase 4 possible. It came from thinking through what it actually means for AI to "learn" code — and realising that the proof isn't in whether the AI writes similar code. The proof is in whether the AI knows **your specific implementation**. The details. The choices. The particular way you solved a particular problem.

If it knows those things — and you can prove your code existed first — that's a story that demands an explanation.
